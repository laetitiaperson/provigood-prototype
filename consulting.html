Frequently asked questions — Consulting

How do I enter the Vietnamese food market? +

Entering the Vietnamese food market typically follows our 4-step approach: understand the market, accelerate growth, optimize operations, and scale sustainably. A typical engagement starts with a focused feasibility study or market survey (1 to 3 months), followed by go-to-market planning, partner identification and operational setup. Provigood handles each step end-to-end or supports your in-house team where you need it most.

What are the legal steps to set up a subsidiary in Vietnam? +

Setting up a subsidiary in Vietnam involves five key steps: choosing the right structure (representative office, branch, WFOE, JV), obtaining an Investment Registration Certificate, obtaining an Enterprise Registration Certificate, completing post-opening formalities (tax, banking, social insurance, seal registration), and ensuring ongoing compliance. Our Corporate Services team handles the full process and works with trusted legal partners to secure the most efficient setup.

How long does a typical consulting project take? +

Timelines vary by scope. A focused feasibility study or market survey takes 1 to 3 months. Legal incorporation of a subsidiary or representative office takes 2 to 4 months. A full end-to-end market entry, from initial study to first commercial activity, typically runs 6 to 12 months. We share a clear milestone plan as part of our proposal.

How are consulting projects priced? +

We use three pricing models: fixed-fee for scoped projects (most common for market entry, M&A, factory setup), monthly retainer for ongoing strategic and operational support, and success-based fees for specific commercial missions. Every proposal includes detailed deliverables, timeline, fees, and assumptions. Time-tracking sheets and expense claims are shared throughout the engagement.
