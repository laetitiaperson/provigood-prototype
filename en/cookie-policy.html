Cookie Policy

Last updated: May 2026

1. What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files that websites place on your device (computer, smartphone, tablet) when you visit them. They allow websites to function properly, remember your preferences, analyze how the site is used, and personalize content.

This Cookie Policy explains which cookies we use on provigood.com, why we use them, and how you can manage them. It should be read together with our Privacy Policy.

2. Cookies we use

We classify the cookies on our Website into four categories. Strictly necessary cookies are always active; all other categories are optional and require your consent through our cookie consent banner.

2.1 Strictly necessary cookies

These cookies are essential for the Website to function and cannot be switched off. They are usually set in response to actions you make, such as setting your privacy preferences, logging in, or filling in forms.

  • Session ID (anonymous session management)
  • Security tokens (CSRF protection)
  • Cookie consent state (remembers your cookie preferences)
  • Language preference (TranslatePress, for switching between English, French, and Vietnamese)

2.2 Analytics cookies

These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our Website by collecting and reporting information anonymously. We use this information to improve our content and user experience.

  • [Tool to be defined, typically Plausible Analytics or Google Analytics]
  • Cookie names and lifetimes depend on the chosen tool. Detailed list will be added once the tool is selected.

2.3 Marketing cookies

These cookies are used to deliver relevant content and to measure marketing campaign performance. They may be set by us or by trusted third-party advertising partners.

  • Currently: none active
  • Possible future additions (only with your explicit consent): LinkedIn Insight Tag, Meta Pixel, retargeting tags

2.4 Third-party cookies (embedded content)

Some pages of our Website include content from third parties (embedded videos, social media). These third parties may set their own cookies, governed by their own privacy policies.

  • Cloudflare: for security, performance, and bot detection
  • YouTube or Vimeo: when video content is embedded
  • LinkedIn, Facebook, and similar: when social media share buttons or embedded content are present

3. Third-party cookies and their policies

You can review the privacy and cookie policies of our third-party providers at the links below:

  • Cloudflare: cloudflare.com/privacypolicy
  • LinkedIn: linkedin.com/legal/privacy-policy
  • Google: policies.google.com/privacy
  • Meta (Facebook, Instagram): facebook.com/privacy/policy

4. How to manage your cookie preferences

4.1 On our Website

When you first visit our Website, a cookie consent banner allows you to accept or reject non-essential cookies. You can change your preferences at any time by clicking Cookie settings in the Website footer.

4.2 In your browser

You can also manage cookies directly through your browser settings:

  • Chrome: support.google.com/chrome/answer/95647
  • Firefox: support.mozilla.org
  • Safari: support.apple.com/guide/safari
  • Microsoft Edge: support.microsoft.com/microsoft-edge
  • Opera: help.opera.com/en/latest/web-preferences

Please note that disabling cookies may affect Website functionality (e.g., language switcher may not remember your preference, contact forms may not work as expected).

4.3 Opt-out tools

For analytics and marketing cookies, you can also opt out using the following industry tools:

  • Your Online Choices (EU): youronlinechoices.com
  • Network Advertising Initiative (US): networkadvertising.org/choices
  • Digital Advertising Alliance (US): aboutads.info/choices

5. Updates to this Cookie Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy to reflect changes in technology, regulation, our practices, or the tools we use. Material changes will be highlighted with a banner on the Website.
