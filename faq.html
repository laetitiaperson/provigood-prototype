Topic 1

About Provigood and Vietnam

What does Provigood do? +

Provigood is a consulting and trading company based in Ho Chi Minh City since 2018. We help food, beverage, FMCG/CPG and hospitality companies grow their business in, from and to Vietnam through three integrated activities: tailor-made strategic consulting (360° market entry, sales, sourcing, M&A), human resources and training under our Provigoodpeople brand, and the distribution of selected products such as Olivo Cold Logistics insulated containers and Em Oi Café compostable coffee capsules. We operate at the heart of a network of 250+ expert partners to deliver excellence with speed and agility.

Why is Vietnam a strategic market for food brands? +

Vietnam is one of the world's fastest-growing markets for FMCG, retail and consumer products. Three factors make it strategic. First, a young, urbanizing population of over 100 million with rising purchasing power and growing appetite for premium and international brands. Second, a privileged trade position thanks to landmark agreements such as EVFTA, CPTPP and RCEP, which open Vietnam to European, North American and Asian exporters under favorable conditions. Third, its role as a manufacturing and logistics hub for Southeast Asia, making it an ideal entry point to the wider region.

Who is Vincent Treluyer? +

Vincent Treluyer is the founder and CEO of Provigood. Originally from Brittany, France, he has 25 years of experience in strategy, business development, F&B, retail and distribution, including more than 20 years in Asia (Taiwan, China, Vietnam). He has held senior positions at major international and local firms such as P&G, Lactalis, Bel, Casino Group and Dai Tan Viet. Based in Ho Chi Minh City since 2009, Vincent is also Deputy Secretary of the Disciples of Escoffier in Vietnam and an active member of the local Franco-Vietnamese business community.

What sectors does Provigood serve? +

Provigood specializes in food, beverage, FMCG, CPG, retail, hospitality and food logistics. We also support adjacent sectors such as pharmaceuticals when projects involve cold chain, sourcing or distribution. Our clients range from international corporations (Panzani, Andros, Neovia/ADM, Lactalis, Carambar & Co, Marou) to growing SMEs and family-owned businesses. Whether you are looking to launch a new brand, set up a factory, build a distribution network, or recruit local talent, our sector-focused expertise ensures your project benefits from deep operational and cultural knowledge of the Vietnamese market.

Topic 2

Consulting & business development

How do I enter the Vietnamese food market? +

Entering the Vietnamese food market typically follows four steps. First, understand the market through tailored research on consumer behavior, trade structure, regulations and competition. Second, accelerate growth with a clear go-to-market strategy, partner identification and brand activation. Third, optimize operations through local sourcing, recruitment and corporate services. Fourth, scale sustainably by building strategic partnerships and securing investments. Provigood guides clients through each step with a tailor-made approach, leveraging local expertise and a 250+ partner network. A typical engagement starts with a free discovery consultation to scope your specific project.

What are the legal steps to set up a subsidiary in Vietnam? +

Setting up a subsidiary in Vietnam involves five main legal steps. First, choosing the right structure (representative office, branch, wholly foreign-owned enterprise, or joint venture). Second, applying for an Investment Registration Certificate. Third, applying for an Enterprise Registration Certificate. Fourth, completing post-opening formalities such as tax registration, social insurance, banking, and seal registration. Fifth, ensuring ongoing compliance with labor, tax and sector-specific regulations. Provigood's Corporate Services team handles the full process and works with our trusted legal partners (including Fidal Asiattorneys) to secure the most efficient setup for your business.

How long does a typical market entry project take? +

Timelines vary by scope and ambition. A focused feasibility study or market survey takes 1 to 3 months. Legal incorporation of a subsidiary or representative office takes 2 to 4 months. A full end-to-end market entry, from initial study to first commercial activity, typically runs 6 to 12 months. Faster trajectories are possible when we leverage existing distributors or franchise structures. Our motto, "Excellence with speed and agility", reflects our commitment to compressing timelines without compromising quality. We provide a clear milestone plan upon receiving your brief.

What are typical salary benchmarks in F&B Vietnam? +

Salaries in Vietnam's F&B sector vary significantly by role, location, company type and experience. As a general guide, an experienced country manager in Ho Chi Minh City typically earns USD 60,000 to 120,000 annually, while sales managers in FMCG range from USD 25,000 to 50,000. Hanoi tends to be slightly lower than HCMC for equivalent roles. Provigoodpeople provides custom salary benchmarks as part of our recruitment and consulting services, including detailed comparisons by sector, function and seniority — essential data for foreign companies sizing their team in Vietnam.

Topic 4

Distribution

How does Olivo Cold Logistics work? +

Olivo Cold Logistics, a French manufacturer founded in 1956 and represented by Provigood in Vietnam, designs passive insulated containers that maintain temperature-controlled goods without mechanical refrigeration. Using eutectic plates or cryogenic sources, Olivo containers preserve cold chain integrity for up to 12 hours (BOX format) or 72 hours (BAC and ROLL formats). They are A.T.P.-certified for the transport of perishables, last over 12 years in operation, and allow multi-temperature deliveries in a single dry truck or motorbike, drastically reducing fuel consumption, CO₂ emissions and last-mile delivery costs.

Are Em Oi Café capsules truly compostable? +

Yes. Em Oi Café and Le Caiffa capsules are fully compostable and made in Vietnam. They are produced by CapsuleCup Ltd. (formerly Bac Tam Tam Ltd.), the first company in Vietnam to design coffee capsules compatible with most capsule machines. The Arabica and Robusta beans are sourced from Dalat, Son La and Dak provinces, then masterfully ground, roasted and packed in Ho Chi Minh City. The capsules are an eco-friendly alternative to aluminum or plastic capsules, biodegrading naturally without harming Vietnamese soils, rivers or ecosystems.

Where can I find your products? +

Em Oi Café and Le Caiffa capsules are distributed in 5-star hotels, premium retailers, wholesalers and select restaurants across Vietnam, Cambodia, Laos and Thailand. They are also available through their dedicated Facebook pages. Olivo Cold Logistics insulated containers are available B2B for food manufacturers, distributors, retailers and logistics operators handling temperature-sensitive products. For pricing, technical specifications, sample requests or distribution inquiries on either product line, contact our team directly via the contact form or by email at contact@provigood.com.

Topic 5

Partnership

What kind of partners do you look for? +

Provigood is always looking for expert partners who can complement our service offering for clients in food, beverage, FMCG and hospitality. We seek collaborators in legal, marketing, sales, logistics, finance, HR, training, quality, sourcing, media, communication, and corporate services. Beyond technical expertise, we look for partners who share our values, integrity, excellence, simplicity, bring complementary strengths, demonstrate a proven track record, commit to mutual growth, and embrace cultural sensitivity to the Vietnamese market. Whether you are a freelance expert, a specialized firm or an institutional player, we welcome a conversation.

How does the partnership process work? +

Our partnership process is structured around five steps. First, an initial consultation to discuss mutual goals, expectations and potential collaboration areas. Second, customized strategy development, where we co-create a working framework leveraging combined strengths. Third, resource allocation, defining roles, responsibilities, and timelines. Fourth, continuous communication through regular check-ins, feedback loops and progress updates. Fifth, performance evaluation to assess outcomes, identify improvements, and adapt the relationship as it grows. The process is collaborative and flexible, designed to build long-term partnerships based on trust and shared vision.

Do you offer commission-based or revenue-share models? +

Yes. Provigood works with several partnership models depending on the type and scope of collaboration. These include strategic alliances for co-developed market entry projects, joint ventures for shared risks and capabilities, distribution partnerships with commission or margin-based models, technology partnerships for integrated solutions, and consultancy collaborations with project-based or revenue-share fees. The exact model is defined during the initial consultation, based on each partner's offering, the project scope, and the value created. Our priority is to design fair and motivating arrangements that drive mutual long-term success.

Working with Provigood
